Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

1630 - 1680

Missile Man of India

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj - The Creator of Purna Swaraj Shivaji , or Sivaji, (born Feb. 19, 1630, or April 1627, Shivner, Pune, India—died April 3, 1680, Rajgarh), Indian king (r. 1674–80), founder of the Maratha kingdom of India. A devout Hindu, he grew up at a time when India was ruled by Muslims, and he found their religious persecution intolerable. Collecting a band of followers, he began in c. 1655 to seize weak outposts of the sultan of Bijapur. In 1659 he lured the sultan’s army to its destruction and, possessed of its horses and armaments, became overnight a formidable warlord. The Mughal emperor Aurangzeb sent out his most prominent general and an army of 100,000 to capture him, but Shivaji made a daring escape. Gaining even more strength, he added a naval force to his military might. In 1674 he proclaimed himself an independent sovereign. He forged an alliance with the sultans in the south, thereby blocking the spread of Mughal rule. His rule was noted for its religious tolerance. See also Maratha confederacy

Achievements and Contributions:

  • Establishment of the Maratha Empire: Shivaji laid the foundation of the Maratha Empire in Western India, which eventually became one of the most significant and enduring powers in the Indian subcontinent.
  • Military Genius: Shivaji was a brilliant military strategist and tactician. He employed innovative guerrilla warfare techniques, including the extensive use of forts and naval power, to challenge the Mughal and Deccan Sultanate rulers.
  • Forts and Fortifications: Shivaji constructed and captured numerous forts, which served as the backbone of his military strategy. He is renowned for his fort-building abilities and the fortifications of places like Raigad, Pratapgad, and Sindhudurg.
  • Religious Tolerance: Shivaji was known for his policy of religious tolerance. He respected and promoted all faiths within his kingdom and ensured that his subjects were free to practice their religions without discrimination.
  • Innovation in Military Technology: Shivaji's army was known for its efficient use of technology and firearms. He encouraged the development and use of advanced weaponry in his military campaigns.
